Explainable AI (XAI) is essential to transition machine learning models from theoretical research into high-stakes, regulated environments. Our research focuses on developing post-hoc and intrinsic interpretability methods to explain decisions made by complex neural networks. We utilize SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) and LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) to construct attribution maps that show feature importances for individual model predictions.

### Active Investigations
- **Real-Time Edge Explanations**: Optimizing attribution calculation times to run on edge gateways and microcontrollers within millisecond latency budgets.
- **Explanation Stability**: Evaluating how explanations shift under distribution noise or adversarial data poisoning.
- **Interpretable Cybersecurity**: Applying feature attributions to network intrusion detection and spoofing detection pipelines.
